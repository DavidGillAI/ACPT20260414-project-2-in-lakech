# Anti-AI Slop Sentiment

The most common complaint about generic AI brand content is that it feels forced, overly polished, emotionally empty, and interchangeable.

## Core insights

- AI content is often rejected when it has no lived experience.
- Common failure modes include generic positivity, overused buzzwords, false emotional intensity, and no specific cultural context or tension.
- "Trying too hard" maps to copy that sounds optimized but culturally unconvincing.
- Pretty language without point of view is a recurring problem.

## Implications

- The brand voice should feel edited by a human with taste, memory, and boundaries.
- Avoid empty optimization language.
- Favor specificity, restraint, and human friction where appropriate.
