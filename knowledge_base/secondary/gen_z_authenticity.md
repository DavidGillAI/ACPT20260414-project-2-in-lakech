# Gen Z Authenticity

Gen Z is highly sensitive to brands that feel rehearsed, overly polished, or socially performative.

## Core insights

- Young audiences value transparency, real-user proof, conversational tone, and genuine purpose.
- Brands that seem like they are trying too hard can be punished.
- Authenticity depends on consistency between message and behavior.
- Real customers, micro-creators, and everyday use cases help validate brand claims.

## Implications

- Express authenticity through documented process and real community context.
- Use a voice that sounds lived-in.
- Avoid exaggerated founder myths, over-scripted activism, and polished brand voice with no human friction.
